Hidden Gems: Underrated Travel Destinations Around the World

Presenting The Hidden Gems Of The World

3 underrated destinations around the world you’ll want to explore

Travel is something that many often do, while most fail to go out for a long time. In both cases, planning plays an important part. No matter how often you travel, or how rarely you travel, picking a particular destination is one of the sole criteria, around which all the planning revolves. It so happens that popular destinations like Paris or Bali often come to mind, when deciding on a place for a trip. However, here we bring to you some of the hidden gems that offer unique experiences without the crowds, and can be explored both by a traveller and a tourist. Let’s take a look at some of the underrated destinations that are worth exploring.

Albania

Overlooked in favor of its neighbors Greece and Italy, Albania boasts pristine beaches along the Albanian Riviera, ancient ruins like Butrint, and warm hospitality. Its affordability and untouched beauty make it a hidden treasure.Albania, on Southeastern Europe’s Balkan Peninsula, is a small country with Adriatic and Ionian coastlines and an interior crossed by the Albanian Alps. The country has many castles and archaeological sites. Capital Tirana centers on sprawling Skanderbeg Square, site of the National History Museum, with exhibits spanning antiquity to post-communism, and frescoed Et’hem Bey Mosque.Poverty in Albania has declined significantly in the last two decades. Yet, with a poverty rate of 20 per cent the country continues to be the poorest country in the region according to the World Bank.

Pyramids of Tikal, Guatemala

Guatemala, with its rich Mayan heritage, stunning landscapes, and iconic historical sites like Tikal, promises a captivating mix of adventure and history. Be sure to visit the picturesque Lake Atitlán, nestled among volcanoes and charming traditional villages.The Pyramids of Tikal in Guatemala are the iconic structures of the ancient Maya city, known for their impressive size and architectural significance. They are located within Tikal National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Tikal is famous for its numerous pyramids, including the twin pyramid complexes and the "temple" pyramids that often marked the burial places of rulers. Pyramid I is topped by the Temple of the Jaguar and rises to 148 feet (45 metres). Just west of Pyramid I and facing it is Pyramid II, standing 138 feet (42 metres) above the jungle floor and supporting the Temple of the Masks. Pyramid III is 180 feet (55 metres) high.

Georgia

Georgia seamlessly combines exquisite wines, ancient fortresses, and the majestic Caucasus Mountains. Its capital, Tbilisi, offers a fascinating fusion of contemporary vibrancy and historic allure. Explore the vineyards of Kakheti or wander through the breathtaking scenery of Kazbegi.Georgia is a country in the Caucasus region, located at the intersection of Europe and Asia, often considered transcontinental. It's bordered by Russia to the north, Turkey and Armenia to the south, and Azerbaijan to the southeast. The capital and largest city is Tbilisi. The official language is Georgian, and the country is known for its rich history, culture, and diverse landscapes, including the Caucasus Mountains and Black Sea coastline.
